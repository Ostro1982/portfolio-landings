Mon · Apr 07 Story · 1,180 chars ● Pending review

The biggest deal we lost last quarter taught me what "compliance-first" actually means.

Three weeks of demos. Two procurement calls. A signed LOI from the Head of Operations. Then Legal killed it on a Friday at 6 PM with a single sentence: "Your data residency story doesn't pass audit."

I was furious. We had the cheapest offer, the best UX, the strongest references. We had built for the buyer. We had forgotten who actually held the veto.

Here's what changed inside Resolve after that loss:

We rewrote the pitch deck around compliance posture, not features. We hired a fractional CISO before our seventh customer. We started every demo with a single slide that says: here's where your data lives, who can see it, and what we'd do if you got subpoenaed.

That deck closes 38% more deals now. Same product. Same team. Same price. We just stopped pretending Legal wasn't in the room.

If you sell into regulated industries in LATAM, the buyer is never the buyer. Build for the gatekeeper.
